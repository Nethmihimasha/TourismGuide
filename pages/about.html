The Pearl of the Indian Ocean

Sri Lanka is an island nation located in the Indian Ocean, renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, warm hospitality, and incredible biodiversity. Despite its compact size of 65,610 km², the country offers a remarkable variety of experiences — from golden beaches and misty mountains to ancient cities and vibrant wildlife.

Through Time

History of Sri Lanka

1500 BC – 1200 AD

Ancient Kingdoms

The island's first great civilizations — Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa — created masterpieces of engineering, including massive irrigation reservoirs and towering dagobas.

21505 – 1948

Colonial Era

Portuguese, Dutch and British colonial powers shaped the island's architecture, language and culture, leaving behind Galle Fort and the plantation economy.

31948

Independence

Ceylon gained independence from Britain on 4th February 1948, becoming one of Asia's first post-colonial democracies.

41983 – Present

Modern Sri Lanka

Following three decades of civil conflict, Sri Lanka emerged as a fast-growing tourist destination, now welcoming over 2 million visitors annually.

Weather

Sri Lanka's Climate Zones

Dry Zone

May – September

North and east regions receive less rainfall. Best for visiting Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa and Trincomalee.

Wet Zone

October – January

South and west coast receive heavy monsoon rains. Perfect timing for east coast beaches and cultural sites.

Hill Country

Year-round cool

Nuwara Eliya and Ella remain cool (15–20°C) throughout the year with misty mornings and refreshing evenings.

Getting Around

Transport in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka offers a variety of transport options for every budget and travel style — from scenic train rides to private chauffeur services.

Scenic Train Journeys

The hill country train from Kandy to Ella is one of the world's most beautiful rail routes, passing through tea plantations and misty mountains.

Best for: Hill country · Budget travellers

Public Buses

An extensive network connects every town and village. Express air-conditioned buses run on major routes between cities.

Best for: Budget travel · Local experience

Tuk-Tuks

Three-wheelers are perfect for short city trips and exploring local neighbourhoods. Always agree on a fare before starting your ride.

Best for: Short distances · City exploration

Private Driver & Car Hire

The most comfortable way to travel. Hire a driver with an air-conditioned car for multi-day tours across the island.

Best for: Families · Comfort · Multi-day tours

Domestic Flights

Cinnamon Air and other operators offer seaplane and domestic flights between Colombo, Sigiriya, Kandy, and the south coast.

Best for: Time-saving · Luxury travel

Airport Transfers

Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) is 35 km from Colombo. Pre-booked transfers, taxis, and ride apps are available on arrival.

Best for: Arrivals · Departures
