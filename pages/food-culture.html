Taste the Flavours of Sri Lanka

Cuisine

A Celebration of Spices & Flavour

Sri Lankan cuisine is celebrated for its rich spices, fresh ingredients, and regional diversity. Influenced by centuries of trade and cultural exchange — from Arab traders to Portuguese, Dutch and British colonials — each meal reflects the country's heritage and generous hospitality.

Traditional Sri Lankan Foods

Rice & Curry

The cornerstone of Sri Lankan cuisine for 2,000+ years

Ingredients: Rice, coconut milk curries, dhal, sambols

📍 Everywhere — every local restaurant

Kottu Roti

Street food icon developed in the 1960s in Colombo

Ingredients: Shredded roti, vegetables, egg, curry sauce

📍 Street stalls, local restaurants

Hoppers (Appa)

Ancient dish with roots in South Indian cuisine

Ingredients: Fermented rice flour, coconut milk, egg

📍 Breakfast spots, village homes

String Hoppers

A breakfast staple dating back centuries

Ingredients: Rice flour noodles steamed in leaf-woven mats

📍 Roadside stalls, homes

Lamprais

Dutch-Burgher community specialty from colonial era

Ingredients: Rice, meat curry, frikkadels — wrapped in banana leaf

📍 Specialty restaurants, Colombo

Watalappam

Malay-influenced dessert from the 18th century

Ingredients: Coconut milk, jaggery, eggs, cardamom

📍 Muslim restaurants, weddings

Pittu

Traditional breakfast from the Northern Province

Ingredients: Steamed cylinders of rice flour and coconut

📍 Northern Sri Lanka, homes

Kiribath

Auspicious milk rice eaten on New Year and special occasions

Ingredients: White rice cooked in rich coconut milk

📍 Homes, temples, celebrations

Celebrations

Cultural Festivals

Vesak

May

The most important Buddhist festival — streets lit with lanterns and pandals. Acts of kindness fill the nation.

Sinhala & Tamil New Year

April 14

Oil lamp lighting, milk rice ceremony and traditional games mark the dawn of the traditional new year.

Esala Perahera

July–Aug

Kandy's magnificent 10-night procession featuring decorated elephants and Kandyan dancers.

Thai Pongal

January

Tamil harvest festival giving thanks to the sun god — households cook fresh pongal rice in clay pots.

Christmas

December

Celebrated warmly across all communities, with churches illuminated and star lanterns on houses.

Ramadan & Eid

Variable

Muslim communities observe Ramadan with fasting and prayer, culminating in Eid celebrations.

Crafts

Traditional Arts & Crafts

Kandyan Dance

A 2,000-year-old classical dance form performed in elaborate costumes for the Esala Perahera.

Devil Masks

Ceremonial Kolam masks carved in Ambalangoda, used in exorcism rituals and folk theatre.

Batik

Intricate wax-resist fabric dyeing art produced throughout the island in vivid tropical patterns.

Wood Carving

Elaborate carvings adorning temple doorways, furniture and decorative panels since ancient times.

Handloom

Cottage industry producing beautiful silk and cotton textiles with traditional geometric patterns.

Ceylon Tea Culture

Sri Lanka is the world's fourth largest tea producer, yielding over 300,000 tonnes of "Ceylon Tea" annually. The hill country estates around Nuwara Eliya produce some of the finest high-grown teas in the world.
